Fred Rogers

  • 1928 - Fred McFeely Rogers was born on March 20 in Latrobe, Pennsylvania
  • 1948 - Transfers from Dartmouth college to Rollins College
  • 1951 - Graduates Rollins College magna cum laude with a bachelor's degree in music composition
  • 1951 - Begins working as a manager at NBC in NYC
  • 1953 - Hired to work in programming at WQED in Pittsburgh, PA
  • 1954 - Fred partners with Josie Carey to write and produce Children's Corner
  • 1959 - Marries Joanne Byrd on June 9
  • 1963 - Fred stars in the Childrens show, Misterogers for Canadian Broadcasting Corporation and is ordained as a Presbyterian minister
  • 1968 - Creates Mister Rogers' Neighborhood
  • 1969 - Testifies in front of the Senate Commerce Committee in support of public television
  • 1979 - Testified in support of home recording at the US Supreme Court
  • 1997 - Is awarded the Television Academy's Lifetime Achievement Award
  • 1999 - Inducted into the Television Hall of Fame
  • 2001 - Fred films the last episode of Mister Rogers' Neighborhood
  • 2002 - Is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President George W. Bush
  • 2003 - Fred Rogers dies of stomach cancer in his home at the age of 74
